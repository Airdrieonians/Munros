Meall Chuaich

Mon 06May19

 

Lesley and I drove from Duloch to Dalwhinnie arriving 1:30PM Left 11:35,

far too late!

 

First time I have climbed a hill starting from our new home in Duloch, Fife.

 

Bad start when I closed the car door on the plastic catch on my waste band of my ruck sack.

It boke in two and I was force to tie it round my waiste instead. hat a pain.

I'll need to buy a new Rucksack too.

 

I brought my bike to help with the long 3 mile leadin path to the hill.

I forgot my chain for security so I took the front wheel off and planked

it part way up the hill.

 

 

This is the most northerly and last of the group of seven arround the

Drumocchter Pass, East of the mighty Loch Etrich.

 

As we parked at layby 94 on the southbound carriageway of the A9,

a walker was ariving back from Meall Chuaich cycling.

He was a fit looking 60 something year old. He told us about the weather

and how his day went.

 

We took the bike and walked 50 yrds north to the field gate at the start

of the 3 mile track which leads to the electic power builing used to maintain

the aquaduct which moved water from Loch Chuaich to Loch Etrich.

It a good quality path used by cars. Good for bike and walking.

It seemed to be downhill surprising ly. On the way back it seemed downhill too.

 

 

I hid my bike minus front wheel at bottom of hill on right by some heather.

Th front wheel I plabked 100 yrds up the hill on the left by a big rock.

 

There was  white wooden sihgn with "Walker Path" painted on it

 

The climbing was steep from the start. Lesley much faster then me.

 

It was sunny at times wih little wind.

 

After a short time it was possible to see the A9 and Loch Erich beyond, even Ben Alder caped with snow.

 

There was some snow on the summit of Meall Chuaich too.

 

We saw big rabbits or Hares now and again.

This is part of a grouse moor, so screaming panicy bird flew off now and again giving us  freit

 

We were getting close to the sholder of the ridge when Lesley ask how long to the top we could see.

I said 25 mins, it took about another hour to get there. There were 3 or 4 false tops on the way.

 

there were light snow showers on our way up, as we neared the top we could see some big black

showers on the way, they passes staright doen th A9 glena dn mised us thankfully.

 

Near the top we spotted a climber comming down far on our left.

That was the onky climber we were to see on the hill all day.

Lesley was ahead and saw the final summit cairn before I did. She did a star jump and pointed.

 

A few minutes later I saw the cairn for myself with some relief.

 

We took pics of Lesley hugging the large round cairn, also on with me star jumping on its wide

circular top.

Munro 65 for me and 3 for Lesley.

Well done both!

 

I had taken 3:45 Hrs to get here. The book said 2:40, so we were slow.

We were 1:30 at the base of the climb where I stowed my bike.

 

It would be 6:33Hrs by the time we got back to the car.

 

I  thought we would get back in half the time but the 3 mile lead in would be little faster going back.

 

On the final stage when

I found and picked up my bike wheel, I was skipping down the slope whith nthe wheel in my hand and

stepped on a collecion of smaller rocks and fell to my left. I felt myself falling and collapsed and

relaxed my left leg. I jarred badly on impact. Lesley was on hnd to console me as I cryed out in pain.

a few minujtes later and I was gingerly on my feet again., but sore.

 

I p;ut the bike back together ahd head to the track.

I noticed I had only one cycle glove.

Lesley suggested going back for it, must have nbeen when I fell.

 

I have not stregth to go back. I'll just by a new pair.
