Ben Buck (Ochil Hills)

Date : Sat 13-Jun-2026

I failed to reach Ben Buck on last year on Fri 18July25 as it was a late afternoon start and I ran out of light.
The tractor track after the sheep pens, was longer than I thought.

This time I started walking from Alva Woodland Park car park at 10:55.
35 min car journey from Fife.

Weather was 15deg with a fare bit of wind at 18mph. from the west.
I was promised sun by the forecast, I never got any.
I got rain instead.

I walked north west from the car park on good path, past the siler mine and burn.

This takes you through a gate to the Zig Zag tractor track.

I get past only one zig zag when very misty light rain starts.
I ignore it and continue.
The rain gets heavier and heavier.
I stop and shelter by the northern clif side of the zig zag.
I put the waterproof over my rucksack and my waterproof jacket.
I side there sheltering for 10 mins.
If the rain doesn't stop in 10 mins I'm going home.

The rain does stop and I put my soacked jacket over my rucksack belt with the 
wet side to the sky to allow it to dry in the wind.

I continue, but if it starts raining again, i'm off home.

I get to the 1st gate by the small wood after the zig zag tiurns into a straight track.

I see a 40-ish woman come down past then two teen age boys.
They survived the rain too.

I pass sheep on the track and get to the sheep pen gate in 1Hr 15 mins.
I got here in 1 hour previously.

The slope is much less now and I power walk without stopping all the way up the glen to the end of the track.
I get to anther gate in 22 mins and to the track end in 2hrs total.

I'm now on the open hilside.
There is a false top which I thought was summit.
Then on further to the real summit with small cairn on a plateu in 2hrs 21mins.

Visibility is poor, cloud blowing over the summit.
I see Ben Cleuch summit momenarily, and the large wind turbines on the opposite side.
I don't hang about. Got my ski poles out and head back into the wing to get back to the glen.
I meet a asian couple om the way up.
They ask how it is up there.
Light rain and wind.

I race back to the track, on the way a mad bird stand in front me and gives a bowing and chirping display.
It is probaly trying to lead me away from it's nest.

I get back to the car in 1:15 on discent. That is a good speed.

3:35 on hill today.
