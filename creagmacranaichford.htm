Creag Mac Ranaich(Corbett by Loch Earn), turned back at the ford over Kendrum burn 

Date : Fri 10-July-2026

25Dec C - Very hot and sunny.

I left Fife 9:10, stopped at Callander then on to LochEarnHead car 
park with toilet of sorts.

Started with bike 11:02.

Cycled to the cycle way by the English Anglican church.

The narrow road was steeper than I thought.
I got off the bike and pushed it to the slipway that took you down 
to the cycle path proper.
I tied it up and started walking.

I was already sweaty and tired.

I stopped in a shady spot on the path and had food and a rest.

I was a tarmaced path, it started to rise with zigzags.

It then flattened to a former train line.
There was a miletone monument thing there, 7 miles to Killin.
I had expected to turn left there as the map said.
But it looked like a wild wood with a gate.
I went the other way looking for the left turn further up.

After 5 mins I realised this was not the way.
I went back to the milestone and tried to get throught the gate.

It was a flat overgrown path , no tarmac. Former train line.

I looked at the map and estimated it should take 15 mins to walk 
to the bridge 
where I was to get off the train track.
The timing worked and I turned left of the track to a slip way that 
rose to the bridge above. Time 12:15pm. 1hr 13mins on the walk

I was now in a small wood with good tracter track, which would take me 
to the bealach at 1900 feet. 
I was finding it slow going. Very hot, not much energy.
I plod on by sheep on the track and the smell of pooh.

I had stomach cramps telling me my body wanted to stop. 

It took 2 hours from the bridge to the big ford over the Kendrum burn.
Far too slow, nowhere near the bealach, far less the summit.

I went a little past the ford , the path got steeper.
I called it a day. 1200 feet only. 
I'm not fit enough to do this hill today, and the heat drains the energy from me. 
3 hours on the hill, I turn back.

I got back to the car in 1 hour 30 mins.

4:30 on hill today.

I might try this corbett from the opposite side next time. 
That route from the north, goes direct on to the hill without the
1 hour pre amle doing it from the Loch Earn side.
